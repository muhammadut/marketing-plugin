# WordStream / LocaliQ — 2025 Home Services Search Ad Benchmarks

## One-line summary
The current empirical baseline for what a Google Ads campaign in Canadian/US home services should expect for CPC, CTR, conversion rate, and CPL — sourced from a sample of 3,211 home-services search campaigns running April 2024 through March 2025.

## Why this tier
Evidence. This is the load-bearing benchmark for the elocal_clone paid program because it is large-n (3,211 campaigns), industry-segmented at the sub-category level (Plumbing vs Garages vs Pools vs Window Cleaning), recent (2024-2025 window), and produced annually by an organization that runs the underlying campaigns at scale (LocaliQ is one of the largest local-business search-ad operators in North America). Treat the headline numbers as the prior the paid-acquisition-lead must beat; if a proposed campaign target is materially worse than these benchmarks the lead must justify why, and if a live campaign performs materially worse it is broken until proven otherwise. Decay note: refresh annually; the report is republished every spring.

## Key concepts
- **Average CPC for home services, 2025: $7.85.** This is the all-categories average; sub-categories vary materially.
- **Best CPC sub-categories:** Construction & Contractors ($5.31), Garages ($5.75), Pools & Spas ($5.81). Worst sub-categories are typically the ones with the highest urgency and emergency intent (plumbing leak, locked out, no heat).
- **Average conversion rate across all Google Ads industries, 2025: 7.52%.** Home-services rates run higher than the cross-industry average because intent on home-service searches is generally transactional.
- **Average CTR for home services, 2025: 6.37%.** Best sub-categories: Window Cleaning (10.04%), Cleaning/Maid/Butler Services (9.01%), Storage (8.32%).
- **Sample composition.** 3,211 US-based search campaigns, April 1, 2024 to March 21, 2025. The sample is US-based — the Canadian translation is "expect Canadian CPCs to run roughly 0.7-0.9x the US numbers in CAD due to thinner competition," but this is a structural inference, not a number from this report.
- **What the report does NOT contain.** Cost per call (only cost per lead-form). Quality of lead. Booking rate. Revenue per call. Network split (Search vs PMax vs LSA). Geo or zip-code performance.

## When to cite
Cited by `paid-acquisition-lead` in every `campaign-structure.md`, `bid-strategy.md`, and `budget-plan.md` as the prior expectation for CPC, CTR, conversion rate, and CPL by vertical sub-category. Cited by `paid-acquisition-researcher` when refreshing `paid-research.md` annually. Cited by `marketing-director` when a sub-vertical lead proposes a budget that contradicts the implied unit economics (e.g., "$1,000 per call target" when the benchmark says CPL is closer to $80-150 in the relevant sub-category).

## Anti-citation guidance
Do not cite this as authority for **Canadian** CPCs without flagging the US sample bias; the structural inference is that Canadian CPCs are lower per click but the empirical confirmation has to come from the project's own Google Ads account once spend starts. Do not cite this for **PMax or LSA** performance — the report covers traditional Search campaigns, not Performance Max or Local Services Ads, both of which behave differently. Do not use the all-categories averages as a target for one specific sub-category when the sub-category number is published — always cite the most specific row available.
