# Demand Curve — Growth Playbooks

## One-line summary
Demand Curve's open playbook library is the modern operator-level reference for paid social creative testing cadence, landing-page conversion rate optimization, and the now-standard "ad libraries → angles → variants → kill-or-scale" workflow that has replaced the older "set-and-forget" Facebook Ads playbook.

## Why this tier
Scaffolding. The Demand Curve playbooks are produced by a paid-acquisition agency-school (founded by Julian Shapiro, who previously ran growth at Webflow); they are operator wisdom synthesized from running thousands of accounts, not academic research. The frameworks predict outcomes about as well as any operator framework — which is to say they are forcing functions, not laws. What they do well: they give a founder a written, repeatable cadence for creative testing and landing-page iteration, which is the work most solo founders skip in favor of "I'll come up with one good ad and run it forever." The cadence is the contribution.

## Key concepts
- **Angles, not headlines, are the unit of paid social testing.** An angle is a complete creative thesis: who the buyer is, what pain you're naming, what promise you're making, what proof you're showing. Two ads testing the same angle with different headlines test nothing meaningful. Two ads testing different angles (problem-aware vs solution-aware, price vs urgency, social proof vs authority) test the angle.
- **Creative testing rhythm: 3-5 net-new angles per week.** Demand Curve's published cadence for accounts with $5k+/mo Meta spend. Less than that and the angle library starves, more than that and you can't read results before kill-or-scale decisions.
- **Kill-or-scale criteria.** A creative is killed when it hits 1.5-2x the account-average CPA without a clear path to improvement. A creative is scaled when it hits 0.7x or better with stable CTR and frequency under 3.0.
- **Ad libraries as research input.** Before writing creative briefs, scrape competitor ad libraries (Meta Ad Library, TikTok Creative Center, Google Ads Transparency Center) to map the angles competitors are running and find the gap.
- **Landing page conversion rate optimization (CRO) hierarchy.** Test in this order: (1) traffic-to-page match (does the page deliver what the ad promised?), (2) above-the-fold message clarity, (3) primary CTA placement and copy, (4) social proof placement, (5) form length, (6) page speed. Anything else is fine-tuning that won't move the needle.
- **PMM angle inventory.** A working library of 20-50 angles a startup keeps in a sheet, each tagged with audience segment, awareness level, and which channels it has been tested on. New ad rounds pull from the library; new winners feed back into the library.
- **Performance creative is not brand creative.** Performance creative is short, testable, ugly, and built to die. Brand creative is long, considered, expensive, and built to live. Confusing the two wastes both budgets.

## Anti-citation guidance
Do not cite Demand Curve as evidence for any specific number (the "3-5 angles per week" figure is operator wisdom, not a research finding — it is a starting point that will adjust based on the account's actual signal density). Do not cite Demand Curve for B2B SaaS enterprise sales playbooks — the playbooks are calibrated for B2C / DTC / SMB SaaS, and enterprise B2B has different rules. Do not assume the cadence applies on a $1k/mo budget — at low spend the variance per ad is too high to read 3-5 angles per week, and the realistic cadence is 1-2 angles per week with longer test windows.
