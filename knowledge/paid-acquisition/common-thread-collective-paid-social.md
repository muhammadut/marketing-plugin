# Common Thread Collective — Paid Social Methodology

## One-line summary
CTC's published methodology for diagnosing and operating Meta and TikTok ad accounts at scale — the more operationally rigorous counterpart to Demand Curve, with a stronger emphasis on the unit-economics math that has to back any "scale this winner" decision.

## Why this tier
Scaffolding. CTC is one of the largest paid-social agencies for DTC brands in North America; their published frameworks are operator wisdom from managing nine-figure ad spend across hundreds of accounts. The frameworks are not research — they are heuristics that work because the team has seen them work across many accounts. What makes them load-bearing as scaffolding: CTC pioneered the discipline of reading every paid-social decision through a unit-economics lens (gross profit per order, contribution margin after ad spend, payback period) rather than through "ROAS as a vibe." The unit-economics gate is the discipline that prevents the most common ad-account failure mode: scaling a campaign that looks great on ROAS but is unprofitable on contribution margin.

## Key concepts
- **The Prophit System.** CTC's branded methodology that anchors every ad decision to gross profit, not ROAS. The math: revenue × gross margin − COGS − ad spend − fulfillment − fixed allocation = contribution. A ROAS of 2.5 might be wildly profitable for one product and bleeding money for another depending on margin structure. The Prophit framing forces the operator to compute the right number, not a flattering proxy.
- **Creative diversification as risk management.** CTC's recurring claim: an account running a single winning creative is one creative-fatigue cycle away from collapse. The portfolio discipline is to always have 5-10 creatives in market across multiple angles, multiple aspect ratios, and multiple lifecycle stages (top of funnel awareness, mid-funnel consideration, bottom-funnel retargeting).
- **Daily / weekly / monthly cadence.** Daily: pacing check, kill clearly broken creatives. Weekly: refresh creative library with new angles, review attribution. Monthly: full unit-economics review, rebalance budgets across product lines, retire underperforming offers.
- **Bid strategy ladders.** Manual CPC for diagnostics, lowest-cost without bid cap for prospecting, lowest-cost with bid cap for controlled scaling, cost-cap for unit-economics-strict scenarios. Each strategy has a named situation where it's right and a named exit trigger.
- **AIDA mapped to Meta metrics.** Awareness → impression and reach. Interest → CTR and outbound CTR. Desire → landing page view and add-to-cart. Action → purchase or qualified lead. The framework forces the operator to diagnose where in the funnel a struggling campaign is breaking instead of just "the campaign isn't working."
- **The 50% rule for scale.** Don't increase a Meta ad set's budget by more than 50% in 24 hours; the algorithm re-enters learning and can crater performance. Scale slowly and steadily, not in one big jump.

## When to cite
Cited by `paid-acquisition-lead` in `bid-strategy.md` (the bid-strategy ladder and the named situation per strategy) and in `budget-plan.md` (the 50% rule and the daily/weekly/monthly cadence). Cited by `creative-ops` when the brief specifies a creative portfolio (5-10 in market) rather than a single hero creative.

## Anti-citation guidance
Do not cite CTC's ROAS-versus-contribution framing without making the math project-specific — the Prophit framing is right, but the right margin numbers depend on the project's actual unit economics, not the DTC defaults CTC uses in examples. Do not cite the 50% rule as an inviolable law — it's a heuristic for the Meta delivery system's learning phase and the threshold has shifted as Meta has changed its algorithms.
