# Backlinko — On-Page SEO and Link-Building Patterns

## One-line summary
Backlinko is the canonical operator playbook for "what a single page should look like to rank in a competitive SERP" plus the now-mainstream Skyscraper Technique for content-led link acquisition — both born from Brian Dean's pre-Semrush blog and now part of Semrush's content library.

## Why this tier
Scaffolding. Backlinko is operator content based on running SEO experiments and watching what works for Brian Dean's own properties; it is not academic research, and many of the on-page rules of thumb (LSI keywords, ideal word count, etc.) have only weak empirical support beyond "this worked for me." What makes it load-bearing as scaffolding is the **on-page SEO checklist** — a concrete, repeatable artifact a founder can apply to every page before publishing — and the Skyscraper Technique as a forcing function for link-worthy content. Both produce work that wouldn't otherwise happen.

## Key concepts
- **The Skyscraper Technique.** Three steps: (1) find a piece of content in your space that has earned a lot of links, (2) build something materially better than it (deeper, more current, better designed, more comprehensive), (3) reach out to the sites linking to the original and offer your version. The forcing function is the requirement to actually build something better, not to clone the original with a different headline.
- **On-page SEO checklist.** A canonical list of per-page elements: target keyword in title (front-loaded), target keyword in URL, target keyword in H1, target keyword in first 100 words, semantic keyword variations in body, internal links to related pages, external links to authority sources, image alt text, schema markup, page speed budget. Apply consistently and most pages will rank for the keywords they're realistically capable of ranking for.
- **Title tag CTR optimization.** Adding numbers (e.g., "7 ways"), brackets ([2025]), and high-intent modifiers (best, ultimate, complete) to title tags has a documented impact on SERP CTR, even when ranking position is constant. Backlinko has published click-through experiments showing 15-30% CTR lifts from title-tag rewrites alone.
- **Pillar pages and topic clusters.** A pillar page is a long, comprehensive guide to a broad topic; cluster pages are narrower deep-dives on subtopics, all linking back to the pillar. The internal link graph signals topical authority. (Note: HubSpot also popularized this structure; Backlinko's contribution was concrete templates.)
- **Link velocity matters less than link relevance.** A handful of links from sites topically aligned with the target page outperform a flood of links from generic high-DA sources. The implication is that outreach should target topically-relevant pages, not just high-DA ones.
- **Refresh, don't republish.** Updating an existing post (fresh data, new examples, current date in title) often outperforms publishing a new post on the same topic because the existing URL has accumulated link equity. Refresh quarterly for high-value pages.

## Anti-citation guidance
Do not cite Backlinko's "ideal word count" or "ideal number of headings" findings as causal — Brian Dean's original studies were correlational scrapes of top-ranking SERPs, not controlled experiments, and have been widely misread as if higher word count *causes* ranking. Do not cite Skyscraper Technique as a guarantee — the technique works best in spaces where outreach is feasible and the existing link surface is rich; it doesn't help in long-tail spaces where the linking universe is small.
